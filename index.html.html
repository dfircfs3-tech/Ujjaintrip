The Journey
Five days.
Two Jyotirlingas.
One unforgettable drive.
From the prehistoric cave paintings of Bhimbetka to the sacred island of Omkareshwar, from the otherworldly Bhasma Aarti at dawn to midnight jalebis in Sarafa Bazaar — this is Madhya Pradesh at its most essential. A road trip that weaves together ancient heritage, living faith, and the most exuberant street food culture in India.

56 stalls of Indore's finest. Dahi bada, sabudana khichdi, bhutte ki kees, shikanji.

Trip Essentials
What you need to know
🛕
Book in Advance
  • Mahakaleshwar Shigra darshan (₹250/pp) — shrimahakaleshwar.com. Skips 1–3 hr queue.
  • Bhasma Aarti (4 AM) — separate booking needed. One of India's most extraordinary rituals.
  • Bhopal hotel with early check-in confirmed — you arrive at 9–10 AM.
🚗
Rental Car
  • Pick up in Bhopal on Day 1 (11 Apr). Zoomcar, Revv or local agencies near Bhopal railway station.
  • Return in Bhopal on Day 4 (14 Apr evening). Avoid one-way fees.
  • Consider 2 drivers for the Delhi–Bhopal overnight stretches.
  • Expect toll ₹600–700 one way on NH 44.
☀️
April Heat
  • Temperatures reach 38–42°C in MP in mid-April. Plan outdoor sites before 10 AM.
  • Carry ORS / electrolyte packets. Stay hydrated.
  • Light cotton clothes. Sun hat strongly recommended.
  • Temples require removing footwear — the stone gets very hot mid-day.
💰
Money & Payments
  • Carry some cash for Joshi Chat House at Sarafa — UPI not accepted there.
  • Bhimbetka entry: ~₹40/person Indian nationals.
  • Sanchi entry: ~₹30/person Indian nationals.
  • Mahakaleshwar Shigra: ₹250/person. Bhasma Aarti booking fee varies.
⚠️
Important Notes
  • Sanchi Stupa is closed on Mondays. Visiting on Saturday (Day 1) is perfect.
  • Omkareshwar boat ride is highly recommended — factor it into your 3–4 hr stay.
  • Mahakal Lok and Kal Bhairav are best at night — plan the Ujjain day accordingly.
  • No non-veg food allowed in the area around Mahakaleshwar temple.
🗺️
Key Distances
